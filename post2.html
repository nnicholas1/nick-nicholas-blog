Why Your Credit Score Matters Before Mortgage Pre-Approval

Welcome back! Today we’re diving into why having a solid credit score is crucial before you apply for a mortgage—and how a higher score can save you big money over time.

Why Credit Really Matters

Your credit score directly impacts the interest rate you’ll qualify for. Even a small bump in your score can lower your rate by 0.25% or more, translating into thousands of dollars in savings over a 30-year loan.

3 Steps to Improve Your Score Fast

  • Pay on time, every time: Late payments hurt your score the most. Set up autopay or calendar reminders.
  • Lower your balances: Aim to keep your credit-card utilization under 30% of each limit.
  • Clean up errors: Order a free credit report at annualcreditreport.com and dispute any inaccuracies.

What to Watch Out For

Avoid opening too many new accounts at once—a flurry of credit inquiries can temporarily ding your score. Also, don’t close old accounts; length of history matters.

By following these steps, you can often see a score increase within 60–90 days—just in time to submit your mortgage application with confidence.
